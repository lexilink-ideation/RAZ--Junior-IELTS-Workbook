Vocabulary

Tap each card to learn the word! 点击单词卡查看意思！
🐥chick(n.)
小鸡▾
Simple meaning
a baby chicken that has just hatched from an egg
Example
The chick broke out of its shell and looked around.
🥚hatch(v.)
孵化▾
Simple meaning
when a baby bird or animal breaks out of its egg and comes into the world
Example
Chicks hatch from eggs after about three weeks.
📦incubator(n.)
孵化器▾
Simple meaning
a special warm machine that keeps eggs at the right temperature so they can grow and hatch
Example
We put the eggs inside the incubator to keep them warm.
✏️mark(v.)
做标记▾
Simple meaning
to draw or write a sign on something so you can tell it apart or remember it
Example
Mark the eggs with an X and an O to tell the sides apart.
🔓crack(n.)
裂缝▾
Simple meaning
a thin break or split that appears in a hard surface, like a shell
Example
After three weeks, we checked the eggs for cracks.
🧼clean(adj.)
干净的▾
Simple meaning
free from dirt or germs; not messy or dirty
Example
Wash your hands to stay clean before touching the eggs.
🌡️warm(adj.)
温暖的▾
Simple meaning
having a comfortable, gentle heat — not cold, not too hot
Example
The incubator helps the eggs stay warm so the chicks can grow.
🏜️dry(adj.)
干燥的▾
Simple meaning
having no water or wetness; not wet
Example
Water in the incubator keeps the eggs from getting dry.
🔄turn(v.)
翻转▾
Simple meaning
to move something so that a different side is facing up or forward
Example
Each day, turn the eggs three times so the chick can move around.
🌾feed(v.)
喂食▾
Simple meaning
to give food to a person, animal, or bird so it can eat and grow
Example
After the chicks hatch, you need to feed them every day.

Story

Read the story. Target words are highlighted. 多读几次！
🐥 A classroom with students gathered around a table looking at small fluffy chicks and eggs
Pages 3–4

Chicks are baby chickens. Chicks hatch from eggs. You can hatch chicks at school.

🧼 A student washing hands at a sink, and another student writing X and O on eggs at a table
Pages 5–6

First, wash your hands. This helps you and the eggs stay clean.

Next, mark the eggs with an X and an O. This helps you tell one side from the other.

📦 A student placing eggs carefully inside a white incubator machine, and another adding a cup of water
Pages 7–8

Then, put the eggs inside an incubator. This helps the eggs stay warm.

Next, put water inside the incubator. This keeps the eggs from getting dry.

🔄 A student gently turning eggs in the incubator; another student looking closely at an egg with a small crack appearing
Pages 9–10

Each day, turn the eggs three times. This helps the growing chicks move around.

In about three weeks, check the eggs for cracks. Finally, the chicks will hatch over the next day or two!

🐣 A fluffy newborn chick standing in a student's hands, and a child offering it food in a small dish
Pages 11–12

Wait one more day before you touch the chicks. Then, feed the chicks. Last, find them a good home.

🧩 Sentence Patterns

Chicks ___ from eggs.
This helps the eggs stay ___.
Each day, turn the eggs ___ times.

📐 Key Grammar

Sequence words: First, Next, Then, Finally, Last — order the steps of a process
Purpose: This helps the eggs stay warm. — "This helps + noun + verb"
Imperatives: Wash, Mark, Put, Turn, Feed, Find — giving instructions

💭 Things to Remember

Chicks are baby chickens that hatch from eggs.
An incubator keeps eggs warm and adds water so they do not get dry.
Steps: wash hands → mark eggs → incubator → add water → turn daily → check for cracks → hatch → feed → find a home!
