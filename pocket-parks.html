Vocabulary

🌳park(n.)
公园▾
Simple meaning
a place outside where people go to play and relax
Example
A pocket park is like a regular park, only smaller.
📦pocket(adj.)
小型的▾
Simple meaning
small; fitting in a very small space
Example
A pocket park can be as small as a parking space.
🟫lot(n.)
空地▾
Simple meaning
a piece of land, often an empty one in a city
Example
An empty lot is a great place for a pocket park.
💧waterfall(n.)
瀑布▾
Simple meaning
a stream of water that falls from a high place
Example
People can relax and listen to the waterfall.
🎨decorated(v.)
装饰▾
Simple meaning
made something look nicer by adding colors or objects
Example
People decorated this pocket park with many lights.
🗿statue(n.)
雕像▾
Simple meaning
a figure made from stone or metal, shaped like a person or animal
Example
An artist made a statue for this pocket park.
😌relax(v.)
放松▾
Simple meaning
to rest and feel calm and comfortable
Example
People can relax and listen to the waterfall.
🌈colorful(adj.)
色彩丰富的▾
Simple meaning
having many bright and different colors
Example
Check out the colorful art at this pocket park.

Story

Read the story. Target words are highlighted. 多读几次！
🏙️ A city street with a small green area — Perk Plaza in Cleveland, Ohio
Pages 4–5
What Is a Pocket Park?

Some cities don't have space for big, grassy parks. That's why they build pocket parks instead.

A pocket park is like a regular park, only smaller. A pocket park can take up a city block. It can also be as small as a parking space.
🔨 Workers and community members building a small park in Detroit, Michigan
Pages 6–7
Lots of Empty Lots

An empty lot is a great place for a pocket park. It can take many workers to build one.

People who live close by can help, too. They can plant flowers or trees. They can also help keep the park clean and safe.
🌊 A beautiful park with a waterfall wall and seating — Paley Park, New York City
Pages 7–8
Different Pocket Parks

Every pocket park is different.

This pocket park has a waterfall at the back. It also has many places for people to sit.
🎨 Colorful murals and a swing set — 24th Street Mini-Park, San Francisco & a light-decorated park in St. Paul
Pages 9–10
Check out the colorful art at this pocket park. It has a swing to play on, too.

People decorated this pocket park with many lights. They twinkle at night.
🗿 A statue of Harriet Tubman — Boston, Massachusetts; a calm pond park in New York City
Pages 11–12
An artist made a statue for this pocket park. It reminds people of someone important.

This pocket park has grass and a large pond. People can come here to think.
🐢 A painted snake and turtle mural — Marsh Park, Los Angeles; trees surrounding a tiny park in Seattle
Pages 13–14
A man paints a big snake and turtle in this pocket park. They look as if they would be fun to play on.

This tiny pocket park is almost hidden in trees. People can relax and listen to the waterfall.
🌍 A map of the United States showing eight pocket park locations
Page 15 — Conclusion
Pocket parks can be found all over.

Do you have a pocket park where you live?

also: "It also has many places for people to sit."
too: "It has a swing to play on, too."

💭 Things to Remember

Pocket parks are very small parks built in cities.
People who live nearby can help build and care for them.
Every pocket park is different — some have waterfalls, statues, or colorful art.
