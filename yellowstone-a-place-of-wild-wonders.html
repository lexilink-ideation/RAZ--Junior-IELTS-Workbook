Vocabulary

Tap each card to learn the word! 点击单词卡查看意思！
💦geyser(n.)
间歇泉▾
Simple meaning
a hot spring that shoots water and steam high into the air from time to time
Example
Old Faithful is a famous geyser that shoots water into the air.
♨️hot spring(n.)
温泉▾
Simple meaning
a place where naturally heated water comes up through the ground
Example
Yellowstone has many beautiful hot springs with bright blue water.
🌋hot spot(n.)
热点▾
Simple meaning
a place in Earth's crust where very hot, liquid rock rises close to the surface
Example
Yellowstone is on top of a hot spot, which creates its geysers and hot springs.
🏞️national park(n.)
国家公园▾
Simple meaning
an area of land protected by the government for its natural beauty, history, or wildlife
Example
Yellowstone became the first national park in the United States in 1872.
🌿native(adj.)
原住民的▾
Simple meaning
originally living in or from a place; the first people, animals, or plants in an area
Example
Native peoples lived in Yellowstone long before it became a national park.
🛡️protected(adj.)
受保护的▾
Simple meaning
kept safe from harm, damage, or unwanted change
Example
The land in Yellowstone is protected, so people cannot build houses there.
🐺wildlife(n.)
野生动物▾
Simple meaning
wild animals and plants that live in nature, not raised by people
Example
More than 350 kinds of wildlife live in Yellowstone, including bison and wolves.
💥erupt(v.)
喷发▾
Simple meaning
when a volcano suddenly sends out hot lava, steam, or ash with great force
Example
The old volcano under Yellowstone doesn't look like it will erupt again soon.

Story

Read the story. Target words are highlighted. 多读几次！
🗺️ A map showing Yellowstone National Park in Wyoming, Montana, and Idaho, with key sites marked
Pages 3–4 · Introduction
Yellowstone is a national park in the western United States. It is famous for its geysers, hot springs, and amazing wildlife. People visit Yellowstone every year to see its natural wonders.
💦 Old Faithful geyser shooting a tall column of steam and hot water high into the blue sky
Pages 5–6 · A Hot Spot
Yellowstone is on top of a hot spot. The hot spot made geysers, hot springs, and hot mud pools. People go to Yellowstone to see Old Faithful. Old Faithful is one of the geysers. It shoots steam and hot water high into the air. Yellowstone has about three hundred geysers. Yellowstone is also home to an old caved-in volcano. It doesn't look as if it is going to erupt again soon.
🦬 A large herd of bison rolling in the dirt on an open meadow, with golden eagles and deer nearby
Pages 7–8 · Wildlife
More than 350 kinds of animals live in Yellowstone. Some animals, like bison, are big and hairy. They roll on the ground to get rid of flies.
🐺 A pack of gray wolves running through a snowy Yellowstone forest, with a grizzly bear in the background
Pages 9–10 · Wildlife (continued)
Grizzly bears sleep in dens all winter. Wolves were once forced out of Yellowstone. People brought them back later. Today, both bears and wolves are protected animals in the park.
📜 Old paintings and sketches of Yellowstone's landscape made by early explorers, next to a photo of Native people
Pages 11–12 · History
Native peoples lived in Yellowstone before it was a national park. Explorers came and made paintings of the land. More people went to Yellowstone and knew it had to be protected. Yellowstone then became the first national park in the United States.
🎣 Families fishing, hiking, and camping beside a crystal-clear Yellowstone lake surrounded by forests
Pages 13–14 · Conclusion
Many people go to Yellowstone every year. They go to fish, hike, and camp in the park. Today, there are many more national parks in the United States and around the world.
