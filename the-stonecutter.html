Vocabulary

Tap each card to learn the word! 点击单词卡查看意思！
⛏️stonecutter(n.)
石匠▾
Simple meaning
a person whose job is to cut or shape stone
Example
Long ago there lived a poor stonecutter in Japan.
🛒merchant(n.)
商人▾
Simple meaning
a person who buys and sells goods to make money
Example
One day, he saw a merchant who had many beautiful things.
👑emperor(n.)
皇帝▾
Simple meaning
a man who is the ruler of an empire — the most powerful person in the land
Example
The emperor had beautiful clothes, and the stonecutter was sad.
✨spirit(n.)
精灵▾
Simple meaning
a magical being that cannot be seen but has the power to make wishes come true
Example
The spirit of the mountain heard him and granted his wish.
🌟wish(n./v.)
愿望／许愿▾
Simple meaning
a strong hope or desire for something to happen; to want something very much
Example
"I wish I were a merchant," he whispered to the mountain.
😊proud(adj.)
自豪的▾
Simple meaning
feeling very happy and pleased about something you have done or who you are
Example
Every day, the stonecutter cut stone from the mountain. He was proud of his work.
😢wept(v.)
哭泣▾
Simple meaning
past tense of "weep"; to cry because you feel very sad
Example
"I want to be a stonecutter again," he wept.
😌content(adj.)
满足的▾
Simple meaning
happy and satisfied with what you have; not wanting anything more
Example
At the end, he became a stonecutter again and was content with his simple life.

Story

Read the story. Target words are highlighted. 多读几次！
⛏️ A poor man in old Japanese clothing cutting stone from a rocky mountain with a chisel and hammer
Pages 3–4
Long ago there lived a poor stonecutter in Japan. Every day, he cut stone from a mountain. The stonecutter was proud of his work. He was very happy. One day, he saw a merchant. The merchant had many beautiful things. The stonecutter was sad. He did not have any beautiful things.
✨ A glowing spirit emerging from the mountain in a flash of light, as the stonecutter stands watching in amazement
Pages 5–6
"I wish I were a merchant," he whispered. The spirit of the mountain heard him. Poof! He became a merchant. A month went by. One day, he saw the emperor. The emperor had beautiful clothes. The stonecutter was sad. He did not have such beautiful clothes.
👑 A man in royal emperor's robes standing proudly, then looking up sadly at the bright shining sun above him
Pages 7–8
"I wish I were the emperor," he said. The spirit of the mountain heard him. Poof! He became the emperor. Two months went by. One day, he saw the Sun shining brightly. Again, the stonecutter was sad. The Sun was stronger than he was.
☀️ A blazing sun in the sky, then dark clouds blown by wind rolling in to cover the sun
Pages 9–10
"I wish I were the Sun," he cried. Poof! He became the Sun. Then wind blew a cloud over the Sun. The stonecutter grew angry.
🌬️ Powerful swirling winds blowing everything except a great mountain, which stands unmoved and solid
Pages 11–12
"I want to be the wind," he yelled. Poof! The stonecutter became the wind, but he was still angry. He could not blow the mountain around. "I want to be a mountain!" he screamed. Poof! He became a mountain.
⛏️ A tiny stonecutter chipping away at the side of a huge mountain, as the mountain-spirit watches sadly
Pages 13–15
The next day, he felt something cut into his side. It was a stonecutter. "I want to be a stonecutter again," he wept. Poof! He became a stonecutter. He was very happy.

Reading Comprehension

Answer the questions about the story.

A · True or False 判断对错

1.The stonecutter was proud of his work and happy at the start of the story.
2.The spirit of the mountain granted the stonecutter's wishes.
3.The stonecutter was always happy after each wish came true.
4.At the end of the story, the stonecutter became the wind forever.
5.The stonecutter wept when he wanted to go back to cutting stone.

B · Multiple Choice 选择题

1.What did the stonecutter wish for first?
2.Who granted the stonecutter's wishes?
3.Why was the stonecutter angry when he became the wind?
4.How did the stonecutter feel at the very end of the story?
5.What is the main lesson of this story?

C · Short Answer 简答题

1.What was the stonecutter's job before he made any wishes?

Hint: Look at page 3. What did he do every day on the mountain?
2.How many wishes did the stonecutter make in the whole story? List the things he wished to be.

Hint: Count each time he says "I wish" or "I want to be." Look through all the pages.
3.If you could make one wish, what would you wish for? Why?

🧩 Sentence Patterns

"I wish I were a ___," he whispered/said/cried.
The spirit of the mountain heard him. Poof!
He became a ___ and he was very happy.

📐 Key Grammar

Simple Past: "He became a merchant." / "He wept."
Wish + were: "I wish I were the emperor." (expressing wishes)
Circular structure: The story begins and ends the same way.

💭 Things to Remember

The stonecutter was never satisfied — he always wanted more.
The spirit of the mountain granted every single wish.
True happiness comes from being content with who you are.
