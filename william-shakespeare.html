Vocabulary

Tap each card to learn the word! 点击单词卡查看意思！
🎭actor(n.)
演员▾
Simple meaning
a person who acts out parts in plays, on television, or in movies
Example
Shakespeare was an actor as well as a writer in London.
📜plays(n.)
戏剧▾
Simple meaning
stories that are performed by actors on a stage
Example
Shakespeare wrote many kinds of plays — some funny, some sad, some about history.
🌟popular(adj.)
受欢迎的▾
Simple meaning
liked or enjoyed by many people
Example
Shakespeare died long ago, but his work is still popular today.
📚history(n.)
历史▾
Simple meaning
the study and story of past events
Example
Some of Shakespeare's plays are about history, like kings of England.
🎬films(n.)
电影▾
Simple meaning
movies; stories told through moving pictures
Example
Many of Shakespeare's plays have been made into films.
🤔difficult(adj.)
困难的▾
Simple meaning
not easy; needing a lot of effort to do or understand
Example
His plays show how difficult — and how beautiful — life can be.
🧑‍🎤characters(n.)
人物▾
Simple meaning
the people in a story, play, or film
Example
People around the world have laughed and cried along with Shakespeare's characters.
📝poems(n.)
诗歌▾
Simple meaning
pieces of writing that use beautiful or unusual language, often about feelings
Example
Shakespeare also wrote many poems — they are mostly about love.

Pages 3–4 · Introduction
William Shakespeare wrote many plays. He died a long time ago, but his work is still popular.
🏙️🎭 A painting of London in the 1500s with the Globe Theatre in the background; an actor on stage
Pages 5–6 · Early Life & London
Shakespeare was born in Stratford, England, in 1564. Many people at that time could not read or write, but Shakespeare could!

Shakespeare was an actor. He also wrote many kinds of plays.
🗡️👗 Elizabethan theater scene with actors in fancy costumes and sword fights on stage
Pages 7–8 · Shakespeare's Plays
In Shakespeare's time, people loved to see plays. They were exciting, with sword fights and fancy costumes. Only men could act. Women weren't allowed to act.

Some of Shakespeare's plays are funny. Some of his plays are sad. Some of his plays are about history.
🌍❤️ Children and actors performing Shakespeare's play Henry V; audiences watching outdoors in Canada
Pages 9–12 · Shakespeare's Importance
For hundreds of years, people around the world have enjoyed Shakespeare's plays. They've laughed and cried along with his characters.

People feel connected to his stories about love, hate, bravery, and power. It doesn't matter if the stories take place today or long ago. Many of Shakespeare's plays have been made into films.
📝⚰️ A page of handwritten Shakespeare poems; a church in England where Shakespeare is buried
Pages 13–16 · Poems, Death & Conclusion
Shakespeare also wrote many poems. They are mostly about love.

Shakespeare died in 1616. His friends made a book of Shakespeare's plays. They made sure people read and saw his plays.

Today, William Shakespeare is thought to be one of the greatest writers of all time. His plays show how difficult — and how beautiful — life can be.

🧩 Sentence Patterns

He died long ago, but his work is still ___.
Some of his plays are ___ and some are ___.
Many of his plays have been made into ___.

📐 Key Grammar

Contrast with "but": "He died, but his work is still popular." — opposites in one sentence
Passive voice: "Many plays have been made into films." — focus on the result
Present Perfect: "People have enjoyed his plays for hundreds of years." — started in the past, continues now

💭 Things to Remember

Shakespeare was born in 1564 and died in 1616 — he lived in Elizabethan England.
Only men could act in Shakespeare's time; women were not allowed on stage.
His stories deal with universal themes: love, hate, bravery, and power — that's why they last.
