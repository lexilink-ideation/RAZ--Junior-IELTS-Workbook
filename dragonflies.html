Vocabulary

Tap each card to learn the word! 点击单词卡查看意思！
🐛insect(n.)
昆虫▾
Simple meaning
a small animal with six legs, three body parts, and usually two pairs of wings
Example
A dragonfly is a flying insect that lives near water.
👥pair(n.)
对/双▾
Simple meaning
a set of two things that belong together or are used together
Example
A dragonfly has two pairs of wings — four wings in total.
🦟adult(adj./n.)
成年的/成虫▾
Simple meaning
fully grown and developed; the final stage in an animal's life
Example
Adult dragonflies have the largest eyes of all insects.
💨breathe(v.)
呼吸▾
Simple meaning
to take air into the body and let it out again through the nose or mouth
Example
Young dragonflies can breathe under the water.
🧭direction(n.)
方向▾
Simple meaning
the way or course toward which something moves or faces
Example
Dragonflies can change direction quickly in the air.
🏹hunt(v.)
捕猎▾
Simple meaning
to search for and catch or kill animals for food
Example
Adult dragonflies hunt small flying insects to eat.
🌪️swarm(n.)
群▾
Simple meaning
a large group of insects moving together at the same time
Example
Sometimes dragonflies travel in large groups called swarms.
📏skinny(adj.)
细长的▾
Simple meaning
very thin and narrow; not wide or fat
Example
A dragonfly has a long, skinny body and large wings.
🎢swoop(v.)
俯冲▾
Simple meaning
to fly downward quickly in a curved path, like a bird diving
Example
Dragonflies can swoop and dive through the air to catch prey.
🤩amazing(adj.)
令人惊叹的▾
Simple meaning
so impressive or surprising that it fills you with wonder
Example
Dragonflies are amazing insects — they can fly in any direction!

Story

Read the story. Target words are highlighted. 多读几次！
🌿 A bright green dragonfly flying fast over a shimmering pond surface
Page 4 — Amazing Dragonflies
Look! Something small and green is flying fast over the water. It's a dragonfly!
🐉 Close-up of a colorful dragonfly showing its large eyes, six legs, long skinny body, and two pairs of transparent wings
Pages 5–6 — What Are Dragonflies?

Dragonflies are flying insects with two pairs of wings. They come in many colors. Dragonflies can be green, blue, gold, or even pink.

Some dragonflies have stripes. Some change color over time. A dragonfly has a large head; a long, skinny body; and two pairs of wings.

💧 Young brown-green dragonfly nymph underwater near pond plants, hiding among reeds and rocks
Pages 7–8 — Young Dragonflies

Dragonflies always live close to water. They lay their eggs in or near water. The young dragonflies live in water for a year or more.

Young dragonflies look different from adult dragonflies. They can breathe under the water. Their green and brown colors help them hide from fish, frogs, and other hungry animals.

👁️ Extreme close-up of a dragonfly's enormous compound eye, showing thousands of tiny hexagonal sections
Page 9 — Super Sight

Adult dragonflies have the largest eyes of all the insects. Each eye is made up of about thirty thousand tiny parts. They can see almost all the way around at once.

✈️ A dragonfly caught mid-flight, wings a blur, hovering above a pond — its flight path looping upward and backward
Page 10 — Amazing Flight

Dragonflies are some of the fastest flying insects in the world. They can change direction quickly in the air. They can fly forward, backward, and even upside down! Dragonflies can swoop and dive through the air.

🏹 An adult dragonfly snatching a smaller insect out of the air mid-flight, viewed from below against a bright sky
Pages 11–12 — Amazing Hunters

Adult dragonflies hunt small flying insects. They catch these insects from behind and below. They often eat their food in the air. Dragonflies eat mosquitos.

🐸 A frog leaping toward a dragonfly on a lily pad, while a bird watches from a nearby branch
Pages 13–14 — Dragonflies for Dinner & Swarms

Birds, lizards, frogs, and spiders all like to eat dragonflies. Dragonflies eat other dragonflies, too. Not many animals can catch a dragonfly!

Sometimes dragonflies travel in large groups. These groups are called swarms.

🌍 A world map with small dragonfly icons scattered over every continent, with a close-up of a stream and pond habitat
Page 15 — Dragonflies Worldwide

There are nearly three thousand kinds of dragonflies. They are found all around the world. Look at your closest stream, pond, or lake. Maybe you can spot some dragonflies!

🧩 Sentence Patterns

Dragonflies are ___ insects with two ___ of wings.
They can change ___ quickly in the air.
Young dragonflies can ___ under the water.

📐 Key Grammar

Superlatives: the largest eyes, the fastest flying insects — comparing using "the ___est"
Can for ability: They can breathe under water. / They can fly upside down.
Adjectives before nouns: small green insect, long skinny body, large compound eyes

💭 Things to Remember

A dragonfly is an insect: 6 legs, 2 pairs of wings, large compound eyes.
Young dragonflies live underwater and breathe through gills; adults fly at high speed and change direction instantly.
Dragonflies are both amazing hunters AND prey for birds, frogs, and spiders.
