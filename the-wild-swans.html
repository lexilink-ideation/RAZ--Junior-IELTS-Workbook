Vocabulary

☠️wicked(adj.)
邪恶的▾
Simple meaning
very bad or cruel; doing evil things
Example
The king married a wicked queen who turned the princes into wild swans.
✨spell(n.)
魔咒▾
Simple meaning
magic words or a charm that makes something happen
Example
The fairy told Eliza that making shirts from nettles would break the spell.
🌿nettles(n.)
荨麻▾
Simple meaning
wild plants with stinging hairs on their leaves — they hurt when you touch them
Example
Eliza picked many nettles, which burned her hands, and made them into yarn.
📋task(n.)
任务▾
Simple meaning
a piece of work that needs to be done
Example
The fairy said Eliza must not speak before the task was done, or her brothers would die.
🌅dawn(n.)
黎明▾
Simple meaning
the time of day when the sun begins to rise — early morning light
Example
At dawn, the eleven princes would become wild swans again and fly away.
⚖️arrest(v.)
逮捕▾
Simple meaning
to catch and hold someone using the power of the law
Example
The king sent out guards to arrest the wicked queen.
🍽️feast(n.)
盛宴▾
Simple meaning
a large and special meal with lots of food
Example
The queen said there would be a feast — roast bird for everyone.
🏰cell(n.)
牢房▾
Simple meaning
a small, locked room where a prisoner is kept
Example
The queen put the swans in a cell under the castle.

Story

Read the story. Target words are highlighted. 多读几次！
👑🦢 A fairytale illustration of a wicked queen casting a spell over eleven young princes who transform into swans
Pages 3–4
There once lived a king who had eleven sons and one daughter. He married a wicked queen who turned the princes into eleven wild swans. The king asked the queen if she knew where the princes were. She shook her head.
🌅🧚 At sunset, eleven swans land and become princes again; at dawn they fly away; a fairy visits Eliza in a dream
Pages 5–6
At sunset, the swans flew to their sister, Eliza. Their feathers fell off, and the eleven princes stood before her. At dawn, they said, they would become swans again.

That night, Eliza dreamed that a fairy came to her. The fairy said to take nettles and make them into eleven shirts. The shirts would break the spell.
🌿🙊 Eliza in the forest picking stinging nettles with burnt hands, silently working on the shirts
Pages 7–8
The fairy also told Eliza not to speak before the task was done. If she did, her brothers would die.

Eliza woke and went to the forest. She picked many nettles, which burned her hands. With the nettles, she made yarn. Then, she made the yarn into shirts.
🦢🕸️ The queen throws a net over the swans; the swans are locked in a dungeon cell under the castle
Pages 9–12
The brothers flew to her each sunset. Each dawn, they became wild swans again and flew away. One morning, the queen saw them leaving Eliza's window and grew worried.

Then the queen said there would be a feast — roast bird for everyone. The next dawn, the queen threw a net over the swans. She put them in a cell under the castle. Only Eliza knew that the birds would be her brothers. She could not speak. She worked instead.
🦢🤴 Eliza throws the nettle shirts over the swans; eleven princes appear; the youngest still has one swan wing
Pages 13–16
The morning of the feast, the queen came for the swans. Eliza threw the eleven shirts over the swans, and they turned into eleven princes. The youngest brother's shirt was not quite done, though. He was left with a swan's wing instead of an arm.

The king sent out guards to arrest the queen. They found a vulture on her throne, but the queen was never seen again.

Reading Comprehension

Answer the questions about the story.

A · True or False 判断对错

1.The wicked queen turned the princes into eleven wild swans.
2.The princes turned back into humans at dawn.
3.Eliza had to make shirts from nettles to break the spell.
4.All eleven princes were completely restored to human form.
5.The king arrested the wicked queen at the end of the story.

B · Multiple Choice 选择题

1.When did the swans turn back into princes?
2.What happened if Eliza spoke before finishing the task?
3.What did the queen plan to serve at the feast?
4.Why did the youngest prince end up with a swan's wing?
5.What did the guards find on the queen's throne at the end?

C · Short Answer 简答题

1.Why did nettles burn Eliza's hands? What does this tell us about Eliza's love for her brothers?

Hint: Nettles have stinging hairs. She kept going anyway. What does that show?
2.Why couldn't Eliza tell anyone that the swans were her brothers, even when they were about to be eaten?

Hint: The fairy gave Eliza a condition: "not to speak before the task was..."
3.Do you think Eliza is a brave person? What would you do if you were in her place?

🧩 Sentence Patterns

At ___, they would become ___ again.
"If she did, her brothers would die."
She threw the shirts over the swans, and they turned into ___.

📐 Key Grammar

Conditional (if/would): "If she spoke, her brothers would die." — a condition and its result
Time clauses: "At dawn, they became swans again." — "at" + time of day
Past Tense narrative: "She picked... she made... she threw..." — storytelling in past tense

💭 Things to Remember

The princes became swans at dawn and turned back into humans at sunset.
Eliza had to stay silent the whole time — speaking would break the condition of the task.
The youngest prince kept one swan's wing because Eliza couldn't finish his shirt in time.
