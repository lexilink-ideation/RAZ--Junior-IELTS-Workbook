😮amazing(adj.)
令人惊叹的▾
Simple meaning
causing great wonder or surprise; very impressive
Example
Sand sculptors make all kinds of amazing statues out of sand.
🏰sand castles(n.)
沙堡▾
Simple meaning
small buildings made of wet sand, often at the beach
Example
You may have seen sand castles at the beach.
🎨sculptors(n.)
雕塑家▾
Simple meaning
artists who shape materials like sand, stone, or clay into art
Example
Some people get paid to play in the sand. They are called sand sculptors.
✂️carve(v.)
雕刻▾
Simple meaning
to cut and shape material carefully to make art
Example
Sand sculptors use tools and their hands to carve the sand.
🗿statues(n.)
雕像▾
Simple meaning
works of art shaped like a person or animal, made from sand, stone, or metal
Example
They are artists who use sand to make interesting statues.
🏆contests(n.)
比赛▾
Simple meaning
events where people do something to try to win a prize
Example
Sand sculptors go to contests around the world.
💰paid(v.)
获得报酬▾
Simple meaning
given money for doing a job or work
Example
Some people get paid to play in the sand.
🏔️giant(adj.)
巨大的▾
Simple meaning
very big and tall; much larger than normal
Example
It takes a lot of work to make giant sand statues.

Story

Read the story. Target words are highlighted. 多读几次！
🏖️ A large, detailed sand castle on a beach — the kind most people have seen before
Page 4 — Introduction
You may have seen sand castles at the beach.

Have you ever seen one like this?
🎨 Professional sand sculptors working on a large, detailed sand creation on a beach
Pages 5–6
Sand Sculptors

Some people get paid to play in the sand. They are called sand sculptors.

Sand sculptors don't just make amazing sand castles. They are artists who use sand to make interesting statues.
⏱️ A sculptor spending many days working on a large detailed sand figure that could last for months
Pages 7–8
What Sand Sculptors Do

Some of their sand statues take days to make. The sand statues can last for months.

It takes a lot of work to make giant sand statues. First, sand sculptors need a big pile of sand.
🪵 Sculptors filling wooden wall frames with wet sand, then jumping on it to pack it down
Pages 9–10
Then they fill wooden walls with sand. They wet the sand as they fill the walls.

Next, they jump on the wet sand to pack it down. This is called a pound-up.
🔨 A sculptor using tools and hands to carve a detailed figure from the top of a sandpile
Pages 11–12
Finally, the sculptors begin to carve the sand. They start carving from the top of their sandpile.

Sand sculptors use tools and their hands to carve the sand. Sand sculptors can carve sand to look like real people.
🦁 Intricate sand sculptures shaped like a city skyline and animals with teeth and claws
Pages 13–14 — Amazing Sand Statues
Sand sculptors can even shape the sand to look like a city. They can make animals that have teeth and claws.

Sand sculptors go to contests around the world. One of the biggest sand sculptures ever built was in China — over 30 sculptors spent 75 days to build one 73 feet tall!
🌊 A sand sculptor finishing a large, beautiful piece — all types of amazing sand art displayed
Page 15 — Conclusion
Sand sculptors make all kinds of amazing statues out of sand.

They all started the same way, though. They started by playing in the sand.

🧩 Sentence Patterns

They are artists who use ___ to make interesting ___.
First… then… finally… (steps in a process)
Sand sculptors can ___ sand to look like ___.

📐 Key Grammar

sequence words: "First… Next… Finally…"
can + verb: "Sand sculptors can carve sand to look like real people."
to + verb (infinitive purpose): "…to make interesting statues."

💭 Things to Remember

Sand sculptors are professional artists who get paid to make sand art.
Making a giant sand statue takes many steps: pile, wet, pack, carve.
