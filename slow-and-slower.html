Vocabulary

Tap each card to learn the word! 点击单词卡查看意思！
🚫avoid(v.)
躲避▾
Simple meaning
to stay away from someone or something on purpose
Example
The Gila monster tries to avoid humans and other animals.
🐛creep(v.)
缓慢爬行▾
Simple meaning
to move slowly and quietly, often close to the ground
Example
At top speed, starfish just creep along the ocean floor.
📅seldom(adv.)
很少▾
Simple meaning
not often; rarely — the opposite of "usually"
Example
Because the sloth seldom moves, little plants grow on its fur.
⚡energy(n.)
能量▾
Simple meaning
the power inside your body that lets you move, work, and stay warm
Example
The Greenland shark must use its energy to stay warm in cold water.
🌙sneaks(v.)
悄悄靠近▾
Simple meaning
to move very quietly so that nobody notices you
Example
The slow loris sneaks up on its food at night without making a sound.
🐾mammals(n.)
哺乳动物▾
Simple meaning
warm-blooded animals with fur or hair that feed milk to their babies
Example
Manatees are large, gentle ocean mammals that float and munch on sea grass.
🏃escape(v.)
逃脱▾
Simple meaning
to get away from danger or a difficult situation
Example
The seahorse can't escape an enemy, so it hides by wrapping around a plant.
🦋glides(v.)
滑翔▾
Simple meaning
to move smoothly and effortlessly through the air or water
Example
The swallowtail butterfly flaps its wings, glides, then flaps again.

Story

Read the story. Target words are highlighted. 多读几次！
🐌🏁 A photo of racing snails with numbers on their backs lined up at a start line
Pages 3–4 · Snail
The animal world has some very slow animals in it. One of the very slowest is the snail. Even the fastest snails are pretty slow. In 1995, Archie the snail took two minutes and twenty seconds to creep 13 inches (33 cm).
⭐🦥 A colorful starfish resting on the ocean floor, and a three-toed sloth hanging upside down in a tree
Pages 5–6 · Starfish & Three-toed Sloth
Snails aren't the only slow animals. Starfish don't seem to move at all. At top speed, they just creep along. A starfish needs four minutes to move the length of a man's arm.

The three-toed sloth hangs around in trees most of the time. Because it seldom moves, little plants often grow on its fur. A sloth needs about three minutes to crawl the length of two cars.
🐢🦎 A giant Galapagos tortoise walking slowly on the islands, and a Gila monster in the Arizona desert
Pages 7–8 · Galapagos Tortoise & Gila Monster
Humans can walk about eighteen times faster than this giant tortoise. The Galapagos tortoise isn't fast but it does live a long time. A tortoise can live 150 years or more.

The desert Gila monster is about as slow as the Galapagos tortoise. It tries to avoid humans and other animals. If scared, it will hiss loudly then bite quickly.
🐠🦋 A tiny seahorse wrapped around a sea plant, and a large yellow-black swallowtail butterfly flying
Pages 9–12 · Seahorse & Swallowtail Butterfly
The bony little seahorse has four small fins but it can't swim fast. It can't escape an enemy — so it hides. It wraps its tail around a branch to look like it's part of the plant.

The large swallowtail butterfly flaps its big wings very slowly. It flaps, glides, then flaps again. It only flaps its wings about 300 times a minute.
🦈🦝 A Greenland shark in dark, icy water, and a slow loris with huge round eyes moving through tree branches
Pages 12–14 · Greenland Shark & Slow Loris
We think of sharks as fast hunters. However, the Greenland shark is the slowest fish in the ocean. It lives in very cold water so it must use its energy to stay warm.

The loris is a careful little animal with big eyes. It sneaks up on its food at night. The sneaky loris moves so slowly through tree branches that it doesn't make a sound.
🐄🌊 A large, round manatee floating gently near sea grass in warm coastal waters
Pages 14–16 · Manatee & Conclusion
Manatees are large, gentle ocean mammals that just float around. Sometimes they are called "sea cows." They like to take their time and munch on sea grass. In an hour of munching and floating, a manatee can go about three miles.

Some of these animals can speed up when they need to. Some are just trying to save their energy. It's true that slow animals don't win many races — still, they have found a way to live that works for them.

🧩 Sentence Patterns

📐 Key Grammar

Comparison: "about as slow as the tortoise" — uses "as...as"
Contrast: "We think of sharks as fast hunters. However, the Greenland shark is..." — uses "However"
Purpose clause: "it uses energy to stay warm" — uses "to + verb"

💭 Things to Remember

Many slow animals move slowly to save energy, not because they are lazy.
The seahorse hides instead of escaping — camouflage is its superpower.
The Galapagos tortoise is slow but can live for over 150 years!
